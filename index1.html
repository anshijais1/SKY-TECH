What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to significant and long-term changes in the Earth's climate patterns, particularly those occurring due to human activity such as the burning of fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes. The primary driver of climate change is the increase in greenhouse gas emissions, such as carbon dioxide (CO2), methane (CH4), and nitrous oxide (N2O), which trap heat in the Earth's atmosphere and cause global temperatures to rise.
